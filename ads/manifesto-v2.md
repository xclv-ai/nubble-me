# NUBBLE.ME — CAMPAIGN V2

## THE MANIFESTO

---

You read a 4,000-word article last week.

You remember two sentences.

Not because you're lazy. Not because you have a short attention span.
Because 3,800 of those words weren't for you.

They were for someone who didn't already know the context.
Or someone who needed every caveat spelled out.
Or someone who learns differently than you do.
But the author didn't know who'd show up.
So they wrote for everyone.

Which means they wrote for no one.

---

This is the dirty contract of reading:
the writer picks the depth, the reader eats it or leaves.

You've been eating it your whole life.
Every textbook. Every report. Every goddamn white paper
that buried its one insight under forty pages of throat-clearing.

You didn't have a choice.

Skim and miss things?
Or read every word and resent most of them?

That's not a reading experience.
That's a hostage situation.

---

We don't accept this anywhere else.

Maps zoom. Photos zoom. Spreadsheets zoom.
You pinch two fingers and the world reorganizes around what you need to see.

But text? Text is still a fixed pipe.
Same words, same order, same length — for the PhD and the intern,
for the expert and the beginner, for the person with five minutes
and the person with five hours.

Everyone gets the same pipe shoved into their brain.

In 2026.

---

**Nubble breaks the pipe.**

Scroll to move through ideas. Swipe to control how deep you go.
Every section. Independently. From one-line summary to full expansion.

Your brain already does this. You already skim parts and slow down on others.
You already know which sections matter to you and which don't.

Now your reader knows too.

---

Read a 300-page book in 90 seconds — not as a summary someone else wrote,
but as the actual book, at depth zero. The skeleton. The nub of every idea.

Then swipe into any section that pulls you. Go as deep as you want.
Or don't. That's the point.

You are not the same reader on page 1 and page 300.
You are not the same reader at 7am and midnight.
You are not the same reader you were a year ago.

Why is your reading experience the same every time?

---

**nubble.me**
Get the nub. Or get the whole thing. Your call.

## ORIGIN STORY AD (Long-form / About page / Brand film script)

Off the coast of Maine, there's a lighthouse called the Nubble.

It sits on a rocky little island — a knob jutting out of the Atlantic. For 150 years, it's been doing one job: pulling signal out of the dark. Telling ships what matters and what to ignore.

The word "nub" has meant "the point, the gist, the heart of something" since 1834. Before that, it just meant a small lump — something you feel under your thumb.

In ancient Egyptian, *nub* meant gold.

We thought that was a good name for a reading app that lets you find the gold in anything. The nub of every section. Or the whole thing, expanded — your choice, your depth, every time.

**nubble.me**
The nub of everything.
