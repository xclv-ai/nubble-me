## THE MANIFESTO

---

**Books have a dirty secret.**

They're the same length for everyone.

The PhD and the curious teenager.
The expert skimming familiar territory and the beginner drowning in new concepts.
The exec who needs the point in 30 seconds and the nerd who wants every footnote.

Same. Exact. Text.

We've had adjustable everything — seats, screens, fonts, even the goddamn room temperature.

But content? Content is still a fixed-width pipe shoved into every brain the same way.

That's not reading.
That's force-feeding.

**Nubble breaks reading into a new dimension.**

Swipe right: more depth. Swipe left: less.
Every section. Independently.

An entire book as summaries in 90 seconds.
The same book, expanded with context, in 4 hours.
Or — the way humans actually read —
skim some sections, dive deep into others.

Your brain already knows what it wants.
Now your reader does too.

**nubble.me**
Reading with a zoom lens.

## PRODUCT HUNT LAUNCH COPY

**Headline:** nubble.me — Reading with a zoom lens

**Tagline:** Swipe left/right to control content depth per section. Read a book as summaries in 3 minutes or expanded in 4 hours. Your brain already knows what depth it wants — now your reader does too.

**Description:**

We broke reading into two axes.

**Y-axis (scroll):** Read through sections vertically. You already know this one.

**X-axis (swipe):** Control depth per section. Summary → Condensed → Standard → Expanded. This is the axis reading forgot.

Every section exists at 4 depth levels. Swipe right for more detail. Swipe left for less. Independently, per section.

An exec skims the intro and dives deep into the methodology. A student expands the unfamiliar sections and summarizes what they already know. A curious reader zooms in wherever they feel the pull.

Same document. Different reading. Every time.

**Built for the way brains actually work:**
- Swipe gestures for per-section depth control
- Keyboard shortcuts (arrow keys + shift for global)
- Pinch-to-zoom on mobile
- Smooth spring animations that feel physical
- Dark mode, reading time that adapts to depth

Currently a web prototype with hardcoded content. iOS app coming — with on-device AI that turns any ePub, PDF, or URL into a nubble. No cloud. No API costs. Runs on Apple's Neural Engine.
